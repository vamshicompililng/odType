1.RICE / 1.ଚାଉଳ

In Odisha, rice is primarily grown during the Kharif season (June to October). It is widely cultivated in states like West Bengal and Andhra Pradesh, where it also has a significant market share. Rice thrives in alluvial soils with good drainage and moisture retention. Optimal soil temperature ranges from 20°C to 30°C. Nutritionally, rice is a staple source of carbohydrates, providing energy. Growing rice typically requires 3-6 months, depending on the variety, with resources including water, fertilizers, and labor. Demand is particularly high in states like Uttar Pradesh and Tamil Nadu, where rice is a dietary staple.

2.ARHAR / 2.ତୀରନ୍ଦାଜ

Arhar, or pigeon pea, is primarily grown in Odisha during the Kharif season (June to October). It is mostly cultivated in Maharashtra and Karnataka, where it has a significant market share. Arhar prefers well-drained sandy or loamy soils with good fertility. The optimal soil temperature ranges from 20°C to 30°C. Nutritionally, it is a good source of protein and dietary fiber. Growing arhar typically takes 5-6 months, requiring moderate resources like water and fertilizers. Demand is particularly high in states like Uttar Pradesh and Madhya Pradesh, where it is a key pulse in diets.

3.MOONG / 3.ମୁଙ୍ଗ

Moong, or green gram, is primarily grown in Odisha during the Kharif season (June to October) and the Rabi season (November to February). It is mainly cultivated in states like Rajasthan and Maharashtra, where it has a significant market share. Moong thrives in well-drained sandy or loamy soils with good fertility. The optimal soil temperature ranges from 25°C to 35°C. Nutritionally, it is high in protein, fiber, and vitamins. Growing moong typically takes about 60-90 days, requiring moderate resources such as water and fertilizers. Demand is particularly high in states like Punjab and Uttar Pradesh, where it is widely consumed.

4.URAD / 4.ଖରିଫ

Urad, or black gram, is primarily grown in Odisha during the Kharif season (June to October) and the Rabi season (November to February). It is mainly cultivated in states like Maharashtra and Tamil Nadu, where it holds a significant market share. Urad prefers well-drained, fertile soils like loamy or clayey soil. The optimal soil temperature ranges from 20°C to 30°C. Nutritionally, urad is rich in protein, fiber, and minerals. Growing urad typically takes about 70-90 days, requiring moderate resources such as water and fertilizers. Demand is particularly high in states like Uttar Pradesh and Karnataka, where it is a staple pulse.

5.MAIZE / 5.ମକା

Maize is primarily grown in Odisha during the Kharif season (June to October) and also in Rabi (November to February). It is most cultivated in states like Karnataka and Andhra Pradesh, where it holds a significant market share. Maize thrives in well-drained, loamy soils with good fertility. The optimal soil temperature ranges from 20°C to 30°C. Nutritionally, maize is a good source of carbohydrates, fiber, and essential vitamins. Growing maize typically takes about 90-120 days, requiring moderate resources like water and fertilizers. Demand is particularly high in states like Maharashtra and Uttar Pradesh, where it is widely consumed.

6.RAGI / 6.ରାଗି

Ragi, or finger millet, is primarily grown in Odisha during the Kharif season (June to October). It is most cultivated in Karnataka, which dominates its market share. Ragi thrives in well-drained, sandy or red soils with good fertility. The optimal soil temperature ranges from 25°C to 30°C. Nutritionally, ragi is rich in calcium, iron, and dietary fiber. Growing ragi typically takes about 90-150 days, requiring moderate resources such as water and fertilizers. Demand is particularly high in states like Tamil Nadu and Maharashtra, where it is valued for its nutritional benefits and as a staple food.

7.WHEAT / 7.ଗହମ

Wheat is primarily grown in Odisha during the Rabi season (November to April). While it is not the leading crop in the state, major production occurs in states like Punjab and Haryana, which dominate the market share. Wheat prefers well-drained, fertile loamy or sandy soils. The optimal soil temperature ranges from 10°C to 25°C. Nutritionally, wheat is a good source of carbohydrates, protein, and fiber. Growing wheat typically takes about 100-120 days, requiring resources like water, fertilizers, and labor. Demand is particularly high in states like Uttar Pradesh and Madhya Pradesh, where it is a staple food.

9.JUTE / 9.ଜଟ୍

Jute is primarily grown in Odisha during the Kharif season (June to October). It is most cultivated in West Bengal, which dominates the market share. Jute thrives in alluvial, well-drained, and fertile soils with high organic content. The optimal soil temperature ranges from 25°C to 35°C. Nutritionally, jute leaves are edible and rich in vitamins, but the primary product is the fiber. Growing jute typically takes about 120-150 days, requiring moderate resources such as water and fertilizers. Demand is particularly high in states like West Bengal and Bihar, where it is essential for packaging and textile industries.
